After a car accident, you're in pain, overwhelmed, and suddenly everyone has advice. Here's the problem: the wrong attorney — or the wrong timing — can slash your settlement by 30–50%.

This guide gives you the insider framework that personal injury attorneys use when *they* get into accidents.

## The First 72 Hours Are Everything

Evidence disappears fast. Surveillance footage gets overwritten. Witnesses forget details. What you do immediately after an accident shapes your entire case.

- Call police and get a report number
- Photograph everything: vehicles, injuries, skid marks, road signs
- Collect names and numbers of all witnesses
- Never give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurer

## Why "No Win, No Fee" Isn't as Simple as It Sounds

Contingency fees range from 25% to 40% of your settlement — and the percentage often *rises* if your case goes to trial. A $200,000 settlement could net you $120,000 after fees and case expenses.

**Ask every attorney:**
- What is your exact fee percentage at settlement vs. trial?
- Are case expenses (experts, filing fees, depositions) deducted before or after your fee?
- Who absorbs costs if we lose?

## The Specialty Trap

General practice attorneys handle everything from wills to DUIs. Personal injury is a specialty. The tactics for maximizing a traumatic brain injury claim are completely different from a broken arm case.

Look for attorneys who handle *only* personal injury and have specific experience with your injury type. Check their trial record — insurers know which lawyers bluff and which ones actually go to court.

## How Insurance Companies Value Your Claim (And How to Influence It)

Adjusters use software (like Colossus) that assigns point values to your injuries. Your medical documentation directly feeds this algorithm.

- See a doctor within 24 hours — gaps in treatment kill claims
- Follow all treatment plans completely
- Document every way the injury affects your daily life in writing

## The "Quick Settlement" Trap

If an insurer offers you money within days of your accident, they're not being generous. They're cutting their losses before you understand the full extent of your injuries.

Some injuries — herniated discs, soft tissue damage, concussions — don't fully manifest for weeks. Once you accept and sign a release, you cannot go back for more.

**Rule:** Never accept a settlement before completing medical treatment and getting a Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI) determination from your doctor.

## Red Flags When Choosing an Attorney

- They guarantee a specific settlement amount
- They pressure you to sign quickly
- They don't return calls personally
- They can't explain their trial history
- No client reviews or only vague testimonials

## What a Strong Attorney Actually Does

A good personal injury attorney reconstructs the accident with experts, deposes witnesses, hires medical specialists to testify on causation, and *prepares for trial* — even if 95% of cases settle. That trial preparation is what pressures insurers into fair offers.

**Your leverage is their fear of a courtroom.** Make sure your attorney creates that fear.
